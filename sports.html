MANCHESTER UNITED AMONG TOP CLUBS EYEING ADEMOLA LOOKMAN

Ademola Lookman’s standout form for Atalanta has placed him firmly on the radar of Europe’s elite, with Manchester United reportedly among several clubs preparing a summer swoop for the Nigerian forward.

Sources suggest that United, alongside Arsenal, Newcastle, PSG, and Barcelona, are keeping close tabs on the 27-year-old after his stellar 2024/25 campaign. Lookman played a crucial role in Atalanta’s impressive season, netting 15 goals and registering five assists in 31 Serie A appearances. He also etched his name into European football history last year with a Europa League final hat-trick against Bayer Leverkusen.

Atalanta have set a valuation of £50 million (€60 million) for the in-form attacker, who is believed to be open to a new challenge. While the Italian club are in no rush to sell, they’re prepared to consider offers that meet their asking price.

Manchester United are expected to bolster their attack this summer under Ruben Amorim, with a focus on adding both a central striker and a dynamic playmaker. Lookman fits the profile, offering versatility, creativity, and a proven goal threat.

Having previously featured in the Premier League with Everton, Lookman brings experience, hunger, and a point to prove, all of which could make him one of the most talked-about names in this summer’s transfer window.

ARSENAL TARGET GIBBS-WHITE TO BOLSTER MILDFIED FIREPOWER

Arsenal are reportedly stepping up their pursuit of Nottingham Forest playmaker Morgan Gibbs-White as Mikel Arteta looks to reinforce his midfield ahead of next season. With Champions League football on offer at the Emirates, the 24-year-old is said to be open to the move, eyeing the next big step in his career.

Gibbs-White delivered a standout Premier League campaign, contributing 8 assists and 9 goals across 34 appearances. His creativity and energy have made him a key figure for Forest and a prime target for top clubs. Arsenal, alongside Manchester City, are believed to be weighing up offers, with Forest reportedly valuing him at £100 million amid concerns over PSR (Profit and Sustainability Rules) compliance.

Former Premier League scout Mick Brown has backed the move, describing Gibbs-White as the “perfect answer” to complement Declan Rice in Arsenal’s midfield. His ability to create chances, dictate tempo, and thrive in high-pressure moments aligns well with Arteta’s vision for a more dynamic and attacking midfield setup.

Gibbs-White has also been linked with a move to Manchester City as a possible long-term replacement for Kevin De Bruyne, after the club pulled out of a potential deal for Florian Wirtz. But with Arsenal showing serious intent, it could be a race between Premier League giants to secure his signature.

With 47 goal contributions in 154 top-flight games and an average match rating of 7.00 this season, Gibbs-White is fast becoming one of the most sought-after midfielders in the league.
